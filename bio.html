Biography

I am a sociolinguist, educator, and visual artist. My academic career bridges qualitative digital ethnography, mixed-methods discourse analysis, and quantitative sociophonetics. I am deeply interested in how marginalized communities navigate grammatical systems, negotiate identity, and engage in creative resistance across digital spaces—particularly within East Slavic language contexts.

As an educator, I bring extensive experience in English as a Second Language (ESL) instruction, translating linguistic insights into practical pedagogical practices. Parallel to my academic work, my background in performance and visual art informs my research framework, driving me to view language not as a static set of rules, but as an actively constructed stylistic bricolage.

Name Pronunciation Notes:
/ɐlʲɪkˈsʲej bɐˈtʲiɕːɪf/

A quick guide for English speakers: "Alexei" sounds roughly like uh-lek-SAY. For my last name, the initial vowel is reduced (buh-), followed by a palatalized "t" and a long, soft "sh" sound (-teesh-sheef).

I am a man of many names, and I also go by Alex or other Russian derivatives of the name, e.g. Alyosha or Lyosha ([ɐˈlʲɵʂə] [ˈlʲɵʂə]. I do not go by Alexie or any form of the name that ends with an [i].

My last name has two palatal(-ized) sounds. That means the body of your tongue is raised against the hard palate. The [ɕ] sound, represented by "shch", is an alveolo-palatal fricative. Since it does not exist in English phonology, I am okay with it being pronounced like [ʃ]. Make sure you stress the second syllable.
